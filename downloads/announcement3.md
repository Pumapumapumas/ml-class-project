Hello Class,

As we move into the most important component of this course, I would like to emphasize the significance of the course project. The project accounts for 60% of your final grade, with the remaining portion coming from the course assignments. There are no exams in this course because machine learning is best learned by applying concepts to solve meaningful problems rather than by memorizing formulas or reproducing algorithms under time pressure.

A common misconception among newcomers to machine learning is that success depends on implementing algorithms from scratch. In professional practice, machine learning engineers rarely write algorithms such as Random Forests, Support Vector Machines, Gradient Boosting, or Neural Networks from the ground up. Instead, they rely on highly optimized and thoroughly tested libraries and frameworks developed by the research and software engineering communities.

The real challenge — and the real value — of machine learning lies elsewhere: solving a problem effectively.
Successful machine learning practitioners must learn how to:
Understand and formulate a real-world problem.
Acquire, clean, and prepare data.
Design an end-to-end workflow or pipeline.
Select appropriate algorithms for different stages of the workflow.
Compare competing approaches empirically.
Tune hyperparameters systematically.
Evaluate results using meaningful metrics.
Analyze errors and refine the solution iteratively.
Communicate findings clearly and professionally.
The Telugu OCR project has been carefully chosen to provide exactly this experience. OCR for a complex language such as Telugu is not a single-algorithm task. It is a multi-stage machine learning workflow that requires image pre-processing, model selection, prompt engineering, evaluation, validation, and iterative refinement. Different techniques and models may be required at different stages of the pipeline, and there is rarely a single “correct” solution.

Your goal is not merely to produce text from scanned images. Your goal is to develop the mindset of a machine learning engineer: to investigate a challenging problem, experiment with alternative approaches, measure outcomes rigorously, and continuously improve your solution based on evidence.

The project also provides an opportunity to experience a reality of modern AI systems: performance improvements often come not from inventing a new algorithm, but from making better decisions about data preparation, model selection, workflow design, evaluation methodology, and parameter tuning.

I encourage you to begin early, work systematically, and view setbacks as learning opportunities. In real machine learning projects, the first solution is rarely the best solution. Progress comes through experimentation, analysis, and refinement.

By the end of this project, you will have gained experience that is much closer to what machine learning professionals do in industry and research environments than any traditional examination could provide.

Please document the approach and iterations. The end result is not as important. You may not have a great solution. This is perfectly fine.

If you are unable to find a team member or would like to work solo, it is fine too.

I look forward to seeing the innovative approaches you develop and the insights you discover throughout this project.
