Final Project — Recorded Presentation Instructions
Hello Class,
This message provides instructions for the presentation component of your final team project.

Recorded Presentation

Since our course is delivered asynchronously, each team is required to record their project presentation rather than present live. This format gives you the flexibility to record at a time that is convenient for all team members.

Please keep the following in mind:

One recording per team. Only a single recorded presentation is needed, regardless of team size.
Team participation is flexible. Whether all team members participate in the recording or a single team member delivers the entire presentation is entirely up to your team. What matters is that the presentation effectively communicates your project work.
Content. Your presentation should cover the key aspects of your project — problem statement, methodology, results, and conclusions. Treat it as you would a live conference or class presentation.
Duration. Aim for approximately 10 minutes. There is no penalty for longer presentations.
Format. Record your presentation as a video file (MP4 preferred) or as a screen recording with audio narration over your slides. Tools such as Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Loom, or OBS are all acceptable.
Submission

Include the recorded presentation in your project directory submission, along with your code, report, and other project documents. If the file size is large, you may upload the video to Google Drive or a similar platform and include the shareable link in your README file.

Deadline

The recorded presentation must be submitted as part of your final project by Thursday, 25 June 2026, 8 pm EST, in accordance with the project submission deadline communicated in the previous message.

If you have any questions, please feel free to reach out.
